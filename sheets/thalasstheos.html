Backstory
Long ago a benevolent tide-goddess walked among mortals — guardian of rivers, lakes, and seas, pouring out healing wherever she stepped — until a great evil from beyond the stars came to unmake the world, and she gave herself to hold it back. Generations later, when she was only a whispered myth, a great storm battered the coast of a small water-genasi community that had never stopped praying to her. When the storm broke, the sea left a glowing seashell on the shore. Inside lay a child: a water-genasi maiden with ocean-flowing hair and luminous, pearl-pale skin — plainly touched by divinity. They took her in and raised her within the Circle of the Land — Coast as Thalasstheos, and she carries the goddess's old kindness into a harder age.

Additional Features & Notes
The healer's resolve. When the company's Tele-Communicator went silent on the road to the Stormy Mountains, she shouldered her pack and walked into the storm alone to find them.
“I am not a resource.” Kind and unbreakable; she heals, holds, and guides — and will not be spent, caged, or bartered.
