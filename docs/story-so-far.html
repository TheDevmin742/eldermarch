Roll Call

Your Company

✦ ═══════════ ❖ ═══════════ ✦

Four strangers bound first by coin, then by fire — a registered guild company, though you have yet to agree on a name.

Wilhelm Thorne
Human artificer & tinkerer
A dour former academy man with a flintlock, a fondness for shiny salvage, and a talent for invention. Reluctant hero; relentless complainer. Built the party's brass “Telecom.”
Kree
Aarakocra rogue
Bright-feathered, quick-fingered, helplessly drawn to coin. Mastermind of the infinite-gold-loop. Carries a black sword that whispers to him — and which he has now drawn, just once.
Thalasstheos
Water genasi, Druid of the Circle of the Sea
Serene, maternal, unbreakably kind — the company's healer and conscience. Joined on the road; earned the company's keep in Phandalin, and is now traveling alone to rejoin you.
Kenra
Kenku, Druid of the Circle of the Moon
Chaos in borrowed voices. Wild-shapes into bear, wolf, and cat. Fell out of the sky and never left. Carries a past in the northern mountains.
Standing: You are registered with the Phandalin Adventurers' Guild — one job a month, or you're disbanded. Your party name is still, officially, “Pending.”

The Road Behind

The Story So Far

✦ ═══════════ ❖ ═══════════ ✦
❖Brindlewick & the Caravan

It began with rain and a robbery. In the little town of Brindlewick, Wilhelm and Kree happened upon two bandits accosting a merchant, Marta Brin, and cut them down. Grateful — and desperate — she hired the pair to escort her caravan through Phandalin Wood for fifty gold. “Bandits have been hitting the lanes for weeks,” she warned. Wilhelm went, in his words, “for the money.”

On the road through the Whispering Pines they shook off a wolf pack, then made camp — only to be ambushed by bandits in the dark. As the fight turned against them, a giant blue bear burst from the mist, scattered the attackers with a wave of water, and became a woman: Thalasstheos, a druid of the sea, who healed their wounds and chose to walk with them.

❖The Ashlings

At an abandoned farmhouse they found roughly forty ashlings — tiny, gentle fire-spirits — led by one called Bop. Driven from their home by fearful farmers, the little creatures needed shelter. Thalasstheos insisted they be invited, not caged: “The world has plenty of destroyers. It could use a few more menders.” So the ashlings rode along in a barrel aboard the wagon. A tense river crossing nearly drowned Wilhelm (the barrel had a bullet hole from the earlier fight), but Thalasstheos lifted them all to safety in a bubble of water.

❖The Troll's Bridge

A new companion fell — literally — from the sky: Kenra, a chaotic kenku druid, who simply decided she was joining. At a fog-choked ravine, a toll troll demanded “Twenty-five gold pieces! Each!” What followed was the legendary infinite-gold-loop: Kree kept pickpocketing the same hundred coins back and re-paying them while Wilhelm distracted the dim creature. It almost worked — until Kree slipped, the gold scattered, and a brutal brawl broke out. The troll beat Wilhelm down before a moonbeam from Thalasstheos, a bear from Kenra, and blades from Kree finished it. Across the bridge, a dead adventurers' chest paid them well.

❖Cinderhearth Manor

The smell of ash led them to a vine-choked manor — once the ashlings' true home, now feverish with fire. Inside waited pit traps, scorched nurseries, and endless fire-spirits. Kree found a glowing red amulet that drank flame whole; Kenra found a magic torch and a strange potion; Wilhelm pocketed a silver candelabra (to Thalasstheos's disappointment — “Their home is sacred. We are guests, not raiders.”). On the ramparts a fire serpent and elemental nearly overwhelmed them, until the red amulet swallowed the blaze entire. Among the rubble, Kree claimed a curious black sword.

Deeper still, a hallway of burning chains barred the way — Kenra drank her red potion, turned fire-proof and glowing, and walked through as a living torch. Beyond lay a chained, smoldering cocoon.

❖The Cindered Salamander

The door slammed; the cocoon erupted in spirits. Wilhelm shot the chains while Kenra and Kree tore them loose — and the cocoon split to reveal an ancient magma salamander, the source of all the fire. It swallowed Wilhelm whole (he stabbed his way out), crushed Kenra, and hurled Thalasstheos aside. From the brink she poured out her last light to revive them all, and together — sword, bullet, dagger, and moonbeam — they brought the beast down. The fire went out for good.

In the quiet after, the calmed ashlings gave their thanks and parting gifts, then melted gently back into the land. The road to Phandalin lay open at last.

❖Arrival in Phandalin

Highwaymen tried the caravan one last time on the approach — Kenra (as a direwolf) sent one fleeing “NOPE — NOPE — NOPE” on horseback. In town, you delivered Marta's goods to the Merchants' Guild and were paid. While you wandered, Kenra met the uncanny fortune-teller Ember at the Shrine of Luck, who spoke of her exile and foretold: “You will be met with reunion.” At a fountain beneath a bronze statue of a four-member adventuring party, Kree proposed you make it official — and you became a company, registering at the Adventurers' Guild with the clerk Casey.

❖A Quiet Night, and a Whisper

You toasted your survival at the inn and took rooms from Frun, the tabaxi innkeeper. Alone in the dark, Kree's pack began to speak — the black sword: “Kree… attune… I am your strength. Your power… and your vengeance.” He refused, and locked it in a drawer.

❖The Robbery & the Hunt

By morning you'd been robbed — Wilhelm's coin and Thalasstheos's satchel gone without a trace, no forced entry, the only clue a scrap of unfamiliar cloth. Frun sighed: “Not the first time this week.” Broke and motivated, you took the one rich contract on the board: displacer beasts ambushing caravans from the mountains. Thalasstheos chose to stay in Phandalin, taking up healing work at a clinic — “You don't need me to take the next step” — while Wilhelm's new Telecom keeps you all in touch. You left by the east gate. In the deep woods, something black flickered, struck Kree, and vanished, leaving only two yellow eyes. “We are being hunted,” Wilhelm said. And somewhere in the trees, something smiled.

❖Into the Woods

Hunting the beasts, you fought running battles through the forest — their flickering projections turning every blow into a guess. You pressed on down the Triboar Trail, wounded and with no Thalasstheos to mend you; once, Wilhelm was hurt so badly that Kenra carried him on her bear-back. A crackling call reached you over the Telecom, and Kree cheerfully swore you were all “fine,” saying nothing of the blood — Kenra playing gently along so as not to worry her. At last the trees broke onto a vast field of reeds, the mountain looming beyond, and you stole a brief rest in the rain: Wilhelm carving dice from deer-bone, Kree turning lazy loops in the drizzle.

❖The Storm in the Reeds

The reeds were a trap. Ambushed again, you lost the Telecom — crushed underfoot by a beast — just as an unnatural lightning storm set the whole field ablaze. Among the flames stalked a scarred alpha, bigger than all the rest. Cornered and desperate, Kree at last drew the black sword: one stroke felled a great beast, the blade flaring purple and pouring strength into him. You barely escaped the burning reeds and reached the cover of the forest, sheltering in an abandoned cabin.

❖The Shape in the Storm

That night, on watch, Kree saw it — a vast winged shape riding the storm itself, lightning and howling wind moving with it like a king's procession, before it vanished into the clouds about the mountain. Far away in Phandalin, the line had gone dead, the clinic was filling with the badly hurt, and the beasts were turning bold. Unable to reach you, Thalasstheos shouldered her pack and set out alone to find you.

Friends & Companions
Thalasstheos en route
The water-genasi druid who saved you on the road and became your healer and heart. Earned the company's keep in Phandalin — and, when the Telecom went silent, set out alone to find you.
Bop & the Ashlings
The gentle fire-spirits you rescued and freed. They gave you parting gifts and faded peacefully back into the land once the manor's fire was ended.

Folk of Phandalin

A red-haired woman in a pointed hat who keeps the Shrine of Luck — and seems to be everywhere at once. She read Kenra's past, foretold a coming “reunion,” gifted Kree a book, and sent Thalasstheos to the clinic. Clearly more than she appears.

The Adventurers' Guild desk clerk who registered your company, handed over the Tablet of Vigor, and set the rules (“one job a month, or you're disbanded”).

The Merchants' Guild trade factor who took delivery of Marta's caravan and paid you. Polite, precise, treats everything as a transaction.
Frun
The tabaxi innkeeper who rents your rooms. Broke the news of the robbery and waived your bill — “On the house.”
Barthen
Kind shopkeeper at Barthen's Provisions. Traded Kree a small bell for a salamander scale; mentioned caravans going missing on the Triboard Trail.
Carmine
The gruff stable-master, with little patience for noise — he has already thrown Kenra out once.
Seb
A young adventurer at the Guild who warned you the displacer-beast contract is nasty work. “You take that, you better be ready.”
Doctor Henning
The hunched, cane-leaning clinic doctor who hired Thalasstheos on the spot. Her base of work while you hunt.
Foes & Strangers

The dim, greedy guardian of the ravine bridge. Victim of the infinite-gold-loop, then of your blades. Dissolved into steam.
The Whisper in the Sword unresolved
Not a person — but a presence. The black blade from the manor speaks only to Kree, offering power and “vengeance” if he will attune to it. So far, he has refused.

The Map So Far

Places Visited

✦ ═══════════ ❖ ═══════════ ✦
Brindlewick
Where it all began — a small, ordinary river town of cobbled streets, bakeries, and woodsmoke, and Wilhelm's home (he taught at its academy). Here the company first crossed paths, saved Marta Brin from bandits, and took the caravan job that started everything.
Cinderhearth Manor
A vine-choked, abandoned estate built around a great central hearth — once the ashlings' home, until an ancient fire stirred beneath it and filled the halls with spirits. You fought through its overgrown courtyard, pit-trapped rooms, burning ramparts, and a hall of molten chains to the chamber far below. With the salamander slain, the manor finally went still, and the land began to take it back.
Phandalin
A frontier trade town with no lord, run by guilds under an old pact — and your home base for now. You've walked its Merchants' Guild and Adventurers' Guild halls, Ember's Shrine of Luck, Barthen's Provisions, Carmine's stables, Frun's inn, and the clinic where Thalasstheos now keeps her work.
The Wild Roads Between
The places of the journey itself: Phandalin Wood and the Whispering Pines, where wolves shadowed the caravan; the fog-choked ravine and the troll's swaying rope bridge; and the dark woods east of Phandalin, where the displacer-beast hunt — and your latest danger — began.

Spoils & Relics

What You Carry

✦ ═══════════ ❖ ═══════════ ✦

Hard-won treasures, heartfelt gifts, and one or two things best handled with care.

Gifts of the Ashlings
Wilhelm — Fireproof Coat
A simple new coat from Bop, untouched by flame. Given after the manor burned out. (He'd lost his old one to fire twice over.)
Kenra — Warm Dark Feather
A feather that still holds a faint memory of fire's warmth.
Thalasstheos — Luminous Pearl
A small pearl that shimmers like moonlight caught beneath water.
Kree — Platinum Coin
A single, perfectly polished platinum coin. In Kree's own words: “an absurd amount of money… I accept.”
Relics & Curiosities
The Black Sword caution
Recovered from the manor and wielded by Kree. It cuts fire-spirits — and now beasts — out of the world for good; in the reeds it felled a scarred alpha clean through, flared purple, and poured strength into him. It whispers for more, and Kree has still not attuned to it.
The Red Amulet
Found in the manor's nursery; it devours flame and fire-spirits whole. It drank the manor's blaze and has since gone quiet. Kree keeps it close.
Tablet of Vigor
Issued by the Adventurers' Guild — one per company, a relic meant to pull someone back from death's door in dire need. “Use it wisely.”
Wilhelm's Telecom destroyed
His hand-built brass communicators, which once kept you linked to Thalasstheos in town — until a displacer beast crushed the field unit underfoot in the reeds. You are now out of contact.
Odds, Ends & Coin
Carried trinkets
Kenra's magic torch and a worn collection of “treasures”; Kree's pouch of manor jewels, his book from Ember, and a little bell; Wilhelm's silver candelabra and tinker's tools. (Kenra's fire-immunity potion was drunk in the manor.)
Note on coin: the Phandalin robbery cleaned out most of your gold — Wilhelm's purse and Thalasstheos's whole satchel were taken. You set out on the hunt functionally broke, which is rather the point of taking it.

Creatures Encountered

A Traveler's Bestiary

✦ ═══════════ ❖ ═══════════ ✦

Not all you have met meant you harm — some you carried, and some carried you.

Ashlings friends
Tiny living embers, no taller than a hand, that glow like coals and chatter in cricket-and-steam squeaks (only Thalasstheos can understand them). Gentle and shy, they nest in warm hearths. You saved a flock of some forty, led by Bop — and they repaid you before fading into the land.
Fire-Spirits
Flickering motes of living flame that infested Cinderhearth Manor — drifting wisps and skittering cinders. Left unchecked they fold into greater horrors: a coiling fire serpent, a towering humanoid fire-elemental, and shrieking swarms of fire-bats. The red amulet devours them; the black sword ends them for good.
The Cindered Salamander slain
An ancient, serpentine beast of molten stone and fire — the source of the manor's curse. It swallows prey whole, breathes gouts of flame, and heals by bathing in lava. The hardest thing you have yet brought down.
Troll slain
A huge, dim, rubber-hided brute that knits its own wounds shut and guarded the ravine bridge for “tolls.” Strong enough to fling a person across a chasm — but no match for a coin scam and four blades.
Wolves evaded
Ordinary pack hunters that shadowed the caravan through the Whispering Pines. You slipped them on a hidden track rather than fight.
Displacer Beasts hunting you now
Sleek, panther-like predators wreathed in illusion — they seem to stand where they are not, so blows meant for them strike empty air. Lashing, tentacled limbs and patient, cruel eyes. Driven down from the mountains, and presently on your trail.

What You Know

Lore of the Ember Road

✦ ═══════════ ❖ ═══════════ ✦
The Ashlings & Cinderhearth Manor
The ashlings once made the manor's great hearth their home — until something ancient stirred beneath it and the fire turned wild. They fled, leaving offerings behind for someone kinder or braver. You were that someone: by slaying the salamander you freed their land, and they reclaimed it before fading away.
Phandalin, a Town of Guilds
A frontier trade town with no lord — ruled instead by guilds under an old pact. The Merchants' Guild governs trade and coin; the Adventurers' Guild handles monsters, ruins, and contracts. You are now sworn members of the latter.
The Threat from the Mountains
Displacer beasts — rare this close to town — have been ambushing caravans coming down from the mountains, choking off trade. No one knows why they've come so far. Stopping them is your charge.

Loose Threads

Open Questions

✦ ═══════════ ❖ ═══════════ ✦

Mysteries you've brushed against but not yet unravelled.

The Whispering Blade
What is the black sword that followed Kree out of the manor? He has drawn it once now — it fed, and it glowed — and it wants more. What happens if he answers its call in full?
The Quiet Thief
Someone robs Phandalin's guests in broad daylight, with no forced entry and never caught. They took your coin and Thalasstheos's satchel. The only clue: a scrap of unfamiliar cloth.
Who Is Ember?
She appears wherever you go, knows what she shouldn't, and speaks in fortunes. Her promise to Kenra — “you will be met with reunion” — still hangs unfulfilled.
The Shape in the Storm
On the worst night of the storm, a vast winged thing rode the wind and lightning over your camp and vanished into the clouds about the mountain. You do not yet know what it was — only that it was enormous, and unafraid.
A Company Without a Name
The guild ledger still reads “Pending.” Sooner or later, you'll have to decide who you are.

The Present Moment

Where You Stand

✦ ═══════════ ❖ ═══════════ ✦

You are registered adventurers of Phandalin, deep in the wilds below the mountain — sheltering in an abandoned cabin after barely surviving the burning reeds.

Your Telecom is destroyed, so you are out of contact with Thalasstheos — who has left Phandalin and is traveling alone to find you.

You have just watched an enormous shape ride the storm to the peak, and the displacer beasts are still hunting. The way forward leads toward the mountain.
