Cinderhearth Manor

The fire-haunted estate the company cleansed on the road to Phandalin — now cooled, quiet, and warmly haunted in the best way.

Cinderhearth Manor is a rural gentry estate, built generations ago by the Brandt family around a single massive stone hearth — the heart the whole house was raised around. By the time the company arrived, the manor was vine-choked, abandoned, and feverish with wild fire. By the time they left, it had cooled and gone quiet, and the ashlings could finally come home.

✦How It Came to Burn

For generations the Brandts kept Cinderhearth in comfort — rural gentry, prosperous and unremarkable. Then magic tore a flock of ashlings out of the Plane of Fire, and the little embers drifted to the manor's great hearth and quietly moved in. The Brandts glimpsed glowing shapes skittering in the walls after dark, took them for evil spirits, and fled — abandoning the house mid-life, portraits on the walls and toys still in the nursery.

For a time the ashlings lived happily in the empty manor, scratching childlike drawings of home onto the walls. Then a Cindered Salamander — a creature whose very nature is to hunt ashlings — found the house and began picking them off. The survivors fled to a drafty barn down the road, where the company later found them. The salamander coiled into the basement's old hearth, spun a cocoon to molt and grow, and bled raw fire-magic into the stone — heat that drew fierce, feral fire-spirits to infest the house.

Then the company arrived, cut through the fire-spirits, shattered the cocoon, and slew the salamander. The fire died. The manor cooled. And forty-some gentle embers came home.

✦The Rooms & Chambers

  • Courtyard & Garden — Overgrown beds and blackened statues; faint ashling footprints near the back wall were the first hint something once lived here.
  • Entry Hall ground floor — A faded red carpet beneath a vaulted ceiling, lined with Brandt portraits whose painted eyes are blackened as if scorched from within.
  • Living Room ground floor — A cold grand hearth and chairs in a half-circle, frozen as if awaiting guests who never returned.
  • Study ground floor — A desk scorched in careful, deliberate lines, and old notes hinting at “the thing beneath the hearth.”
  • Bedroom ground floor — An ash-stained bed frame, the air oddly warm and nostalgic; ashling drawings of family and hearth scratched into the walls.
  • Hallway second floor — Creaking boards with glowing cracks spidering through them, heat rising from below.
  • Nursery second floor — Toys, blankets, and cookware lovingly preserved — where the childlike ashlings nested most tenderly.
  • Collapsed Balcony second floor — The railing fallen away onto the ravine; cold air clashing with the heat within.
  • Cellar basement — Stone steps into the dark, the air turning to hot metal and ash.
  • Old Hearth Hall basement — Blackened walls, a floor warm to the touch, and massive chains vanishing into the ceiling below. The chains were meant to bind, not protect.
  • The Great Hearth Chamber the depths — A vast circular vault built around the manor's original hearth, where the salamander's chained, smoldering cocoon hung pulsing like a heart — and where the fire was finally ended.

✦Creatures of Note

The Ashlings (and Bop)Gentle living embers, no taller than a hand, displaced from the Plane of Fire. They glow like coals, speak in cricket-and-steam squeaks (only Thalasstheos understands them), and nest in warm hearths. Shy and childlike; their leader — insofar as embers are led — is Bop.
Feral Fire-SpiritsWild flames drawn by the cocoon's raw power — drifting wisps and skittering living cinders that, unchecked, merged into worse: a coiling fire serpent, a towering elemental, shrieking fire-bat swarms. NOT the gentle ashlings — the difference matters.
Magma MephitsLesser elementals that took up residence in the cellar as the heat spread.
The Cindered SalamanderThe apex of the manor — an ancient, serpentine creature of molten stone and fire that hunts ashlings by its very nature. It swallowed prey whole, breathed gouts of flame, and healed in lava. Slain by the company after it burst its cocoon.

✦What the Company Carried Out

  • The Red Amulet carried by Kree — Found in the manor; it devours fire and fire-spirits whole. It drank the manor's blaze and has since gone quiet.
  • The Black Sword carried by Kree — Recovered from the rubble; it destroys fire-spirits permanently, and it has begun to whisper. Where it came from, nobody yet knows.
  • The Ashling Hearth-Charm from the nursery — A gentle relic prized by the ashlings; once a day it lets its bearer ignore environmental fire.
  • Brandt valuables — A silver candelabra, a pouch of jewels, and household treasures abandoned in the family's flight.

✦Connected Places

  • The Barn down the road — The drafty, abandoned farmstead where the fleeing ashlings sheltered from weather, mice, and farmers — and where the company first met Bop.
Forty-one ashlings, as of this spring. Nobody counts the shy one under the stair. Ember does.
