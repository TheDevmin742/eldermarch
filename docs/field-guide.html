Beasts, Foes & Friendly Folk of the Road
⚔   ❖   ⚔
Pinned up in the Ironbound Hall for every chartered company that walks out the door. The Guild keeps the heavy ledgers — the exact teeth and tallies — but a body in the field rarely has time to read a ledger with something chewing on it. So this is the short version: what shrugs off your blows, what hurts it worst, the one nasty trick to watch for, and how the folk who lived through it say to fight it. Add to it as you learn. The best entries in this guide were written in somebody's bad handwriting, right after they nearly died.

Volume One — The Hills, the Road & the Manor
WolvesBeast Foe / Friend
Lean, smart, and almost never alone. The danger was never one wolf — it's the other four you didn't count.
DefensesNo notable resistances or weaknesses — beat it like any beast.
Watch forThey hunt as a pack: while two of them flank you, both strike with the upper hand, and a solid bite will put you flat on your back.
Field tipDeny them the flank — fight in a doorway or with your back to stone so they can't circle. Thin the pack fast; most break and scatter once enough of them are down. And remember Kenra wears this shape more often than not — count noses before you loose an arrow.
Bandits & BrigandsHumanoid Foe
Desperate folk with stolen steel. No tougher than you are — which is precisely why they travel in mobs and shoot from cover.
DefensesNone — they bleed like anyone.
Watch forNumbers, crossbows loosed from the treeline, and the occasional captain who hits twice and can turn a blade aside.
Field tipPut the leader down or rattle their nerve and the rest usually lose theirs. These are also the foes you can talk — bluff, bribe, intimidate, or take them alive. Not every fight has to be a fight.
AshlingsEmber-Spirit (Elemental) Friend
Small wordless souls of living ember, drifting through the warm bones of Cinderhearth. They ask nothing and harm no one.
Defenses Immune · Fire Vulnerable · Cold Vulnerable · Water
Watch forNothing hostile — this entry is here so no one swings first. They are gentle, curious, and easily hurt.
Field tipThe only "tactic" is kindness. Keep them warm, keep cold and water well away, and they'll light your path and press small gifts into your hands. An ashling never forgets a mercy.
TrollGiant Foe
The reason every clever party keeps a torch lit. You can cut it down all day; it simply gets back up, wounds closing as you watch.
Defenses Heals every round Vulnerable · Fire Vulnerable · Acid
Watch forIts flesh knits back together each round — blades and arrows barely matter on their own. Only fire or acid shuts the healing off. Three vicious attacks a turn besides.
Field tipYou cannot win this with steel alone. Someone must land fire or acid every round, and the killing blow must be fire or acid — drop it any other way and it rises again in moments. Sort out who carries the flame before the fight, not while it's standing over you.
The Fire of CinderhearthBound Flame (Elemental) Foe
The thing that slept beneath the old hearth and at last wore the shape of flame and walked. It burns what it touches and the room besides.
Defenses Immune · Fire Resists · ordinary weapons Vulnerable · Cold Vulnerable · Water
Watch forBurning it does nothing — it is the burning. It sets the floor alight, scorches anything that strikes it bare-handed, and grows where there's fuel to feed on.
Field tipWater and cold are the answer — water actively wounds it. This is Thalasstheos's element if she's near. Bring an enchanted blade if you mean to trade blows, and never corner it in a room full of timber and oil.
SalamanderFire-Serpent (Elemental) Foe
A coil of living heat from somewhere far hotter than here. To touch it is to be burned; to be caught in its tail is worse.
Defenses Immune · Fire Resists · ordinary weapons Vulnerable · Cold
Watch forIts body sears anyone who grabs or strikes it close, and its long tail can wrap you up and squeeze, holding you fast against the heat.
Field tipCold is its undoing — lean every frost-spell and chilled blade you have into it. Magic weapons cut where plain steel skips off. Keep out of reach of that tail, and don't waste a single spark of fire on it.
Volume Two — The Storm-Crowned Peak
Displacer BeastsMonstrosity Foe
A great panther-thing with two lashing tentacles and a liar's gift: it is never quite where your eyes swear it is.
Defenses Displacement — strikes miss it  No elemental weakness.
Watch forIt throws a false image of itself, so blows aimed at it go wide more often than not. Two raking tentacles. The storm-touched ones up the mountain blink a short step aside the instant they're hit.
Field tipThe illusion drops the moment you land one true hit — so the second it flinches, everyone pile on before it shimmers back. Even better: anything that forces it to save (a gout of flame, a binding vine, a moonbeam) ignores the trick entirely, because you're not aiming at the lie.
Seen it before: Thalasstheos shot the false images out of the air on the trail — if one "wolf" or "beast" bursts like a struck reflection, the real one is somewhere you're not looking.
Storm-Touched LizardsMonstrosity Foe
Once-dull crag-lizards, swollen and maddened by the singing crystals until lightning runs in their throats. They come in swarms and they come from every angle.
Defenses Shrug off · Lightning  Strongest near the crystals.
Watch forThey spit cracking bolts of lightning, scuttle up walls and across ceilings, and a few have a habit of overcharging and bursting when they've spat one bolt too many. The closer to the stormglass, the worse they fight.
Field tipA draught of lightning resistance turns their main weapon into a parlor trick — you found a flask of exactly that, so save it for these. Spread out so one bolt can't catch the whole party, keep an eye up, never stand beside one that's been spitting nonstop, and drag the fight away from the crystal veins if you possibly can.
The Behir — “Skathrix”Monstrosity Foe
A river of blue-black scale and too many legs, long as a barn and twice as patient. The crowned predator of the lower halls — and, troublingly, clever enough to talk.
Defenses Immune · Lightning  No known weakness — this one's earned the hard way.
Watch forIt bites and constricts, can swallow a person whole, scales sheer stone like a lizard on a wall, and breathes a long line of lightning that fills a corridor end to end. It is unhurt by lightning of any kind.
Field tipEvery lightning trick you own is wasted on it — hit it with anything else. Spread wide so its breath can't catch you in a line. If it swallows someone, that's survivable — cut your way out from the inside (Kree will vouch). It loves tight tunnels where it can pin you, so drag it into open ground. And it talks: it can be stalled, deceived, or warned off at least as readily as fought.
