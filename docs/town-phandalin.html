The Eldermarch Gazetteer

Phandalin

A frontier trade town run by guilds rather than lords — practical, hopeful, and suspiciously peaceful. The company's home base.

Phandalin sits where old roads meet new ones — once a mining settlement, now a trade waypoint that survives by organization rather than glory. Guild signs hang from weathered beams; notices are nailed over older notices; everyone here is working toward something, or away from something. There is no noble in charge. Instead the guilds govern, and the peace holds — quietly, and almost suspiciously well.

This is the company's home base: where they registered as adventurers, met Ember, were robbed in broad daylight, and took the displacer-beast contract that sent them into the woods.

✦The Guilds & the Compact

Phandalin runs on a guild-charter system, with disputes settled by the Town Compact — an agreement signed decades ago after a brief, ugly trade war. Membership isn't mandatory, but life is harder without it. Four guilds matter:

  • The Merchants' Guild “Profit Endures” — The real power in town: trade, caravans, warehousing, coin, and contracts. Headquartered at the Ledgerhall (stone, barred windows, a too-loud bell), where trade factor Harlan Voss treats everything as a transaction.
  • The Adventurers' Guild “Risk, Properly Accounted For” — Monster contracts, ruins, escorts, and bounties, run from the Ironbound Hall — old timber braced with steel, trophies everywhere, a bronze statue of four triumphant adventurers out front. House rules: one job a month or you're disbanded; “don't die”; “your funeral.” The job board is the town's pulse.
  • The Scriveners' Circle “Knowledge Is a Resource” — Maps, records, identification of magic, and lore, kept at the Inkspire — narrow, tall, smelling of dust and vellum.
  • The Craftsmen's Consortium “What Breaks Can Be Made Better” — Smiths, tinkers, masons, and alchemists at the Hammerline — constant noise, flying sparks.

✦Places of Note

  • The Square — The town's heart: a trickling fountain, the worn statue of Phandalin's founder, and — before the Adventurers' Guild — a bronze statue of four adventurers raised in triumph.
  • The Shrine of Luck — Ember's candlelit nook tucked between buildings; charms on strings, true fortunes within.
  • The Mage's Library — A tall, quiet building lined with old books, the air humming faintly with something arcane.
  • The Guild Halls — The Ledgerhall (Merchants'), the Ironbound Hall (Adventurers'), the Inkspire (Scriveners'), and the Hammerline (Craftsmen's).
  • Barthen's Provisions · Lionshield Coster · the Alchemist's Nook — General goods; arms and armor; potions and oils.
  • The Stonehill Inn · the Ember Mug — The town's main lodging (Frun's place); and the rowdier tavern — dice, smoke, rumors, and shady contacts.
  • The Sleeping Giant Taproom — The rough miners' tavern, all spilled ale and harder looks — the shady counterpart to the cozy Stonehill.
  • Carmine's Stables · the Clinic — Where the cart-team is kept; and the modest, respected clinic near the shrine where Thalasstheos works.
  • Townmaster's Hall — The civic hall under the Compact — permits, proclamations, and the official “nothing to see here.”
  • Tresendar Manor — A half-ruined old manor on the east edge with deep cellars. Locals steer clear of it.
  • Alderleaf Farm — The halfling Alderleafs' tidy farmstead at the east edge — a warm meal and an open barn door for strays of every kind.
  • The Miner's Exchange (ruined) — The old mining-trade hall, half-fallen now the boom is over — the faded story of why Phandalin exists at all.
  • The Bell Tower — Phandalin's old alarm-bell, rung for fire or raid; a lonely warden keeps it.
  • The Old Shrine (abandoned) — A forgotten shrine that predates the Shrine of Luck — shunned, overgrown, and quietly avoided. No one remembers why.
  • The East & West Gates — The controlled crossings over the stream, watch-logs and all. The east gate opens toward the mountains.

✦Lore & Sayings

  • The Town Compact — Guild self-rule born from an old trade war — why Phandalin has no lord, and why everyone defers to charters and ledgers. Coin creates order; order keeps the monsters out.
  • Bread outlasts silver — The Alderleaf saying that survived the mine — from the winter the family fed the miners when the silver ran thin.
  • The founding four — The bronze adventurers before the Guild Hall represent a celebrated company from Phandalin's past. Nobody agrees on their names — a story waiting to be dug up.

✦Talk of the Town

Heard on the job board and in the taverns lately:

  • The Quiet Thief — Daylight robberies with no forced entry and no culprit — the company's own coin among the takings. “Not the first this week,” they say. The wardens are stumped.
  • A missing trapper — Haran Oakstump, overdue from the wood.
  • Vanishing caravans — Wagons keep going missing on the Triboard Trail.
  • Strange lights — Seen at an old manor outside town — though the company may know more about that than most.
  • Odd jobs — An herbalist wants an escort to gather rare moss; a collector seeks pre-Phandalin map fragments; whispers of brigands on the roads; a hunter who never came back from the hills.
